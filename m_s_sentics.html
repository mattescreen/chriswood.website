matte_screen: SENTICS (2019-20)

for performer, audience, elastic pressure instrument, live electronics, ambisonic audio, and lights

dramaturgy in collaboration with Courtney Mackedanz

matte_screen (m_s, they/them) is the embodiment of a cyber-identity born of the animated code written by artist Chris Wood. m_s seeks the feeling of being in a body, personally and socially, in order to find a sense of authentic social interaction/comm-unity.

Through 02: SENTICS, m_s seeks to re- assess, negotiate, and embody the complex potential of screen-based emotional stimulus. They do this via an elastic pressure-movement instrument and a ritualistic continuation of the research of cyborgologist Manfred Clynes.

m_s undergoes their invented process of machine-mirror-learning via their Essentic Ritual: the body (personal and collective) is the object of encoding, undergoing an instrumental practice of integrating complex internet-derived-stimulus through the activation of space (physical sound) and body (physical movement).

matte_screen: SENTICS will be performed as part of the Emotional AI Symposium March 27-29 2020 at ZKM Karlsruhe. The ritual was developed during the residency "Emotional Artificial Intelligence" by the ZKM | Hertz-Lab at the ZKM | Center for Art and Media, Karlsruhe. This project takes place within the framework of the INTERFACES project with the support of the Creative Europe funding programme of the European Union.
